#Leaving Corporate Behind Moving From Employee To Founder
### Hi, my name is Alan - Co-founder of Spectrum Tuition - Also, do some other stuff in the start-up space - Co-organise FinTech Melbourne, Bitcoin Melbourne, Board of BAA and subcommittee lead for FA on digital currency and blockchain technology - I'm passionate about community and learning
##My (Corporate) story - I'm a recovering tax lawyer - Did Commerce/Law, MCom (Econ) - and almost an LLM - Did international tax law, M&A, structured finance stuff at a Big 4 - Left after 4 years to continue building something I love ####Have Never looked back
##Spectrum Tuition - **Then**: 2 students, 2 co-founders, 2 rooms (sort of a campus) - **Now**: ~450 students, 30 tutors, 3 campuses (have sold one too) and an online education business - **BUT** that's been 13 years in the making!
##Enough About Me...Let's Talk About Building Stuff
##Let's Start With Why It's Awesome - No more dealing with people 'passing time' - You create your own destiny - Building stuff is fun and *REWARDING* - Nothing better than building things that people **USE AND LOVE** - A real chance to make a difference
#BUT...
##It's Not All Rainbows And Skittles - It's **HARD**, damn hard and doesn't get easier - It's super lonely (even if you have a co-founder) - It becomes your **LIFE**. You literally, dream/have nigthmares about it most nights - For a while you are **EVERYTHING** from the cleaner to sales person - Everything is your fault
#So Why Do It?
## Because you're passionate about solving a REAL PROBLEM...
##It also helps if people will pay you to solve it :)
##So What Should You Consider Before Making The Leap? - Directionally, think about what you'd love to spend your time doing - Be prepared to spend nights and weekends proving out your idea - It's on you (i.e there's no one else to blame)
##When Is The Right Time To Leave? - When you have something you're passionate about spending every single day working on and it shows 'enough promise' - When you have momentum - Practically: get your 'house in order' and do the maths
##Next (Small) Steps - Build a network - Allocate time to your ideas - test whether you have the stamina to actually do it - Start testing ideas
